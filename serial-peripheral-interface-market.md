<p><p>Serial Peripheral Interface (SPI) is a synchronous serial communication protocol used for short-distance communication between devices, commonly in embedded systems. <p><p>The Serial Peripheral Interface (SPI) market can be categorized into Active Mode and Passive Mode. In Active Mode, devices generate signals and control data transfer, enhancing speed and efficiency for high-performance applications. Conversely, Passive Mode involves devices that respond to external signals without generating them, typically used for simpler applications where low power consumption is crucial. These market types cater to diverse needs, from high-speed communication in complex systems to minimal power usage in basic devices.</p></p>
