<p><p>The green renewable methanol market primarily consists of two types: bio-methanol and e-methanol. Bio-methanol is produced from biomass or organic waste materials through gasification or fermentation processes, leveraging carbon-neutral feedstocks. E-methanol, on the other hand, is synthesized by utilizing renewable electricity to convert CO2 and water into methanol, often employing techniques like electrolysis and carbon capture. Both types play a crucial role in reducing greenhouse gas emissions and supporting the transition to sustainable energy solutions.</p></p>
