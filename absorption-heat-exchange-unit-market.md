Type I Heat Pumps typically utilize ammonia-water as the refrigerant, making them suitable for industrial applications where high-temperature heating is required. In contrast, Type II Heat Pumps commonly use refrigerants like lithium bromide-water, ideal for low to medium-temperature applications, such as residential and commercial cooling and heating. Both types capitalize on energy-efficient heat transfer processes to provide sustainable solutions.</p></p>
