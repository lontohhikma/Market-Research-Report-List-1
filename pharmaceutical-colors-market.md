<p><p>The pharmaceutical colors market encompasses three main types: synthetic, natural, and natural-identical colorants. Synthetic colors are chemically manufactured, offering consistency and a wide range of hues, often used for their stability and vibrant shades. Natural colors are derived from plant, animal, or mineral sources, appealing to consumers seeking organic options. Natural-identical colors replicate the molecular structure of natural colors but are produced synthetically, providing similar visual appeal while often being more cost-effective and stable in formulations.</p></p>
